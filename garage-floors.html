Why Polyaspartic Beats Regular Epoxy for Missouri Garages

Most homeowners search for "epoxy garage floors" — totally fine. But what we actually install is a polyaspartic/polyurea hybrid system, which outperforms traditional epoxy in every measurable way.

  • UV Stability: Standard epoxy yellows. Our polyaspartic is fully UV stable — color stays true for 15+ years.
  • Temperature Flexibility: Missouri's freeze-thaw cycles crack traditional epoxy. Polyaspartic moves with your concrete.
  • Cure Speed: Drive on it in 24 hours. Traditional epoxy takes 3–7 days.
  • Strength: Up to 6× stronger abrasion resistance — critical for hot tire pickup.
  • Chemical Resistance: Oil, gas, brake fluid, degreasers — our topcoat resists them all.

Installation Process

How We Install
Your Garage Floor

Every install follows the same 5-step process. No skipped steps, no shortcuts — that's why our floors last 15+ years when others fail in 2–3.

1
Full Diamond Grinding

We grind the entire surface with professional diamond grinding equipment — removing coatings, oils, and imperfections. This creates the mechanical profile needed for a permanent bond. This step alone separates professional installs from failures.

⚠️ Most installers skip this. We never do.
2
Crack Repair & Leveling

All cracks, control joints, and surface damage are filled and leveled before coating. We fix problems — we don't coat over them.

3
Primer Coat Application

A penetrating primer seeps into the concrete pores to create a permanent mechanical and chemical bond between the concrete and the coating system.

4
Base Coat + Decorative Layer

Polyaspartic base coat applied evenly across the entire floor. While wet, your chosen decorative system is broadcast in — color flakes, metallic pigments, or solid color — to create your unique look.

5
UV-Stable Topcoat Seal

Final UV-stable, chemical-resistant polyaspartic topcoat locks in the decorative layer. Walk-ready in hours, drive-ready in 24. Backed by written lifetime warranty.

Garage Floor FAQ

How long does an epoxy garage floor last?▾
With professional polyaspartic coatings and full diamond grinding prep, expect 15–25 years. Our system is backed by a written lifetime warranty against fading, peeling, and yellowing on every install.
How much does an epoxy garage floor cost in Osage Beach, MO?▾
A typical 2-car garage in the Lake of the Ozarks runs $1,800–$2,800. 3-car garages run $2,600–$3,800. Free on-site quotes with no pressure — call 417.255.6890.
How long before I can drive on my new garage floor?▾
With our polyaspartic system, foot traffic in hours and vehicle traffic within 24 hours of installation.
Why do some garage floor coatings peel and fail?▾
The #1 cause is skipping diamond grinding. Every Shipp-Mates Epoxy of the Ozarks install starts with full diamond grinding — no exceptions.
Can you coat a garage floor with cracks?▾
Yes. Minor cracks are filled as part of our standard process. We assess significant structural cracking during your free quote.
Is a polyaspartic floor slippery when wet?▾
Our full flake system creates a textured, slip-resistant surface that actually improves traction vs bare concrete. Smooth systems can include anti-slip additives on request.
Can you install in winter in Missouri?▾
Yes, in most cases. We install year-round. Concrete surface temperature must meet a minimum threshold — we evaluate every project.
